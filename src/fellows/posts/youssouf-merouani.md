Specializes in patent databases using NLP and machine learning. Researches women inventors and innovation networks during French industrialization. His dissertation focuses on the origins of the gender patenting gap in France, building extensive patent databases covering 390,000 French patents and 255,000 unique inventors from 1791 to 1900. His work employs natural language processing and machine learning techniques to analyze patent documents, uncovering patterns of innovation and the role of women in technological progress during the nineteenth century.
